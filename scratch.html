Why Basketball?

Basketball has been part of my life for as long as I can remember. Growing up in Queen Creek, Arizona, the sport was always around — pickup games in driveways, rec leagues, and late nights at the gym. It's more than just a game to me. It's one of the best ways I know to connect with people, compete, and have fun all at once.

Even as a D1 Track & Field athlete at BYU, basketball is still my go-to when I have free time. I'll show up to any gym, with anyone. No matter who's playing, I'm in.

What I Love About the Game

Basketball has layers — here's what keeps me coming back:

  1. The Competition
    • Every possession matters — nothing is ever guaranteed
    • You have to be mentally tough, not just physically tough
    • Winning a close game with friends is one of the best feelings
  2. The Culture
    • Basketball is universal — you can play it anywhere in the world
    • The trash talk (respectful, of course) adds to the experience
    • Pickup ball teaches you more about people than almost anything else
  3. The Athletes
    • Watching elite players is genuinely inspiring as an athlete myself
    • The combination of speed, strength, and IQ is unlike any other sport
    • Every era has its GOAT debate — and that's half the fun
  4. The Analytics
    • I actually find the stats side of basketball pretty interesting — who's efficient, who's not
    • Being Pre-Business at BYU makes me think about the NBA as a business too — contracts, trades, market size
    • Sometimes the numbers tell you things your eyes don't catch watching the game
